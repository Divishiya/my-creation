Bridging the Gap between Generations

The generation gap refers to the cultural, social, and ideological differences that exist between people of different generations. These differences arise because each generation has been influenced by different experiences, events, and technologies. To be more precise, younger generations might have grown up with the internet and social media as a regular part of their lives, while older generations may have grown up without those technologies. As a result, there may be differences in values, attitudes, behaviours, and communication styles between different generations, leading to misunderstandings and conflicts.
